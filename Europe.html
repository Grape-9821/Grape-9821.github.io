James Watt

January 19, 1736 - August 25, 1819, Scotland

Inventions:

  • Improved steam engine
  • Separate condenser
  • Rotary engine

Facts

The power unit “watt” is named after him.

Impacts

Sparked the Industrial Revolution, transforming transportation and manufacturing across Europe.

Guglielmo Marconi

April 25, 1874 - July 20, 1937, India

Inventions:

  • Wireless telegraphy
  • Radio transmission system
  • Radio antenna designs

Facts

Sent the first wireless signal across the Atlantic Ocean in 1901.

Impacts

Laid the groundwork for modern communication — from radio to Wi-Fi.

Alexander Fleming

August 6, 1881 - March 11, 1955, Scotland

Inventions:

  • Discovery of penicillin
  • Lysozyme enzyme
  • Antibacterial research methods

Facts

His discovery of penicillin was accidental — from mold growing in his petri dish.

Impacts

Revolutionized medicine by introducing antibiotics, saving millions of lives.

Johannes Gutenberg

Around 1400 - February 3, 1468, Germany

Inventions:

  • Printing press
  • Movable type system
  • Oil-based ink

Facts

His printing of the Gutenberg Bible was the first mass-produced book in Europe.

Impacts

Made books affordable and knowledge accessible, fueling the Renaissance and Reformation.

Louis Pasteur

December 27, 1822 - September 28, 1895, France

Inventions:

  • Pasteurization process
  • Vaccines for rabies and anthrax
  • Germ theory of disease

Facts

A chemist who changed biology forever.

Impacts

Saved countless lives and became the foundation of modern microbiology and immunology.
