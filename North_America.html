Garrett Morgan

March 4 1877 - July 27 1963, United States

Inventions:

  • Three-position traffic signal
  • Safety hood (gas-mask prototype)
  • Sewing-machine improvements

Facts

Used his own smoke hood to rescue men trapped in a gas-filled tunnel in 1916.

Impacts

Improved public safety worldwide, influencing modern traffic-light and rescue-equipment designs.

George Washington Carver

1864 - January 5 1943, United States

Inventions:

  • Crop-rotation system
  • Peanut-based products
  • Sweet-potato by-products

Facts

Developed 300+ uses for peanuts, but never patented most to keep them free for farmers.

Impacts

Transformed Southern U.S. agriculture and promoted sustainability for poor farmers.

Grace Hopper

December 9 1906 - January 1 1992, United States

Inventions:

  • Computer compiler
  • COBOL programming language
  • Debugging concepts

Facts

Found a moth in a computer relay and coined the term “bug.”

Impacts

Shaped modern software engineering and influenced the entire computer industry.

Guillermo González Camarena

February 17 1917 - April 18 1965, Mexico

Inventions:

  • Color-wheel television system
  • Simplified bicolor TV adapter
  • Portable color camera concept

Facts

Built his first working TV set at age 15 using spare radio parts.

Impacts

His color TV technology became the basis for modern broadcasting in Latin America and beyond.

Thomas P. Lecky

December 31 1904 - December 24 1994, Jamaica

Inventions:

  • Jamaica Hope cattle breed
  • Jamaica Red cattle breed
  • Jamaica Black cattle breed

Facts

Developed tropical cattle that thrived in Jamaica's climate and produced high milk yields.

Impacts

Revolutionized Caribbean agriculture and boosted the region's dairy industry.
