Alberto Santos-Dumont

July 20 1873 - July 23 1932, Brazil

Inventions:

  • 14-bis (heavier-than-air aircraft)
  • Airship “No. 6” (lighter-than-air craft)
  • Internal-combustion engine for aviation use

Facts

A lunar crater is named after him — he is the only South American so honoured by the International Astronomical Union.

Impacts

Helped advance early aviation and public demonstrations of flight; influenced aerospace development in South America.

Augusto Ulderico Cicaré

May 25 1937 - January 26 2022, Argentina

Inventions:

  • CH-1 helicopter (first South American-designed helicopter)
  • CH-10 / CH-11 ultralight helicopters
  • SVH-3 flight simulator

Facts

Built his first four-stroke engine at age 11 and converted a car engine to run on propane when he was a teenager.

Impacts

Pioneered helicopter and aviation design in Latin America; supported regional aerospace engineering growth.

Pedro Ruiz Gallo

June 24 1838 - April 24 1880, Peru

Inventions:

  • Monumental clock of Lima
  • Designs for flying-machine (mechanical propulsion)
  • Torpedo / naval ordnance for Peru's military engineering

Facts

Died in an explosion while working on an experimental torpedo in a workshop near Callao.

Impacts

Early contributions to aeronautics and military engineering in South America; influenced local engineering education and national pride.

Carlos Albán

April 9 1844 - January 20 1902, Colombia

Inventions:

  • Zeppelin/metal-casing airship system
  • Trifocal light mirror
  • Universal time clock / pneumo-barometer apparatus

Facts

Held patents in multiple countries (Colombia, Germany, Italy, France, USA) for his inventions.

Impacts

Contributed to early aeronautical and optical engineering in Colombia; inspired cross-border scientific patenting in Latin America.

Domingo Liotta

December 29 1924 - August 31, 2022, Argentina

Inventions:

  • Total artificial heart (implantable)
  • Advanced heart-lung machines
  • Cardiac assist devices for bypass surgery

Facts

Helped pioneer one of the first successful artificial-0heart implants in 1969.

Impacts

Advanced cardiac surgery and medical device innovation in Latin America; helped save lives and improve global medical engineering.
