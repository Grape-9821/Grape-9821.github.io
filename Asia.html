Zhang Heng

78 CE - 139 CE, China

Inventions:

  • Seismoscope (earthquake detector)
  • Water-powered armillary sphere (astronomical device)
  • Improved inflow water-clock mechanism

Facts

Was a poet and government official in the Han Dynasty.

Impacts

Improved early methods of detecting earthquakes and observing celestial objects, paving the way for later developments in geophysics and astronomy.

Ching Wan Tang

July 23 1947 - Present, Hong Kong - USA

Inventions:

  • Organic Light-Emitting Diode (OLED)
  • Organic heterojunction photovoltaic cells
  • Thin-film organic electronics

Facts

Grew up in a small farming community in Yuen Long, Hong Kong, and built his first makeshift tool in the rice fields before becoming a leading inventor

Impacts

His OLED invention transformed display technology (smartphones, TVs) by making screens thinner, more efficient, and higher quality; this underpins a multibillion-dollar industry.

Yoshiro Nakamatsu

78 CE - 139 CE, China

Inventions:

  • Floppy Disk
  • PyonPyon Jumping Shoes
  • Cerebrex Chair

Facts

Develops ideas underwater, believing oxygen deprivation helps creativity.

Impacts

Inspired Japan's creative innovation culture, influencing both technology and design thinking.

Ajay Bhatt

September 6, 1957 - Presnt, India

Inventions:

  • Universal Serial Bus (USB)
  • PCI Express interface
  • Thunderbolt technology

Facts

Though he co-created USB, it was made royalty-free so anyone could use it worldwide.

Impacts

Standardized computer connectivity globally, making plug-and-play devices a universal feature of modern computing.

Hiroshi Amano

September 11 1960 - Present, Japan

Inventions:

  • Blue LED
  • White LED
  • GaN-based optoelectronic devices

Facts

Won the 2014 Nobel Prize in Physics for creating the blue LED, enabling bright and efficient white light.

Impacts

Transformed lighting and display technology, drastically reducing global energy use and powering modern LEDs in homes and screens.
