Kia Silverbrook

1958 - Present, Australia

Inventions:

  • Memjet high-speed ink-jet printing technology
  • Liquid crystal display (LCD) innovations
  • Interactive “Netpage” digital pen & paper system

Facts

Holds thousands of patents globally (over 4,700 U.S. utility patents as of 2022).

Impacts

Has influenced printing, digital media, electronics and patent-intensive innovation across multiple industries.

Tom Denniss

February 24 1961 - Present, Australia

Inventions:

  • Wave energy extraction technology
  • Ocean-energy conversion systems
  • Advanced mathematical modelling for renewable systems

Facts

Set a world record for fastest circumnavigation of the Earth on foot.

Impacts

Contributed to the renewable energy sector, especially ocean power technologies, aligning Oceania with global green-innovation trends

Richard William Pearse

December 3 1877 - July 29 1953, New Zealand

Inventions:

  • Monoplane aircraft with a bicycle-engine design
  • Bicycle with rod-and-rack gearing system
  • Various mechanised farm-equipment innovations

Facts

Some New Zealand sources claim his machine may have flown months before the Wright brothers' 1903 flight.

Impacts

His work showcases early aviation innovation in Oceania and inspires regional pride in home-grown engineering ingenuity.

John Kenton Britten

August 1 1950 - September 5 1995, New Zealand

Inventions:

  • Britten V1000 racing motorcycle with revolutionary materials & design
  • Lightweight engine systems and composite structures
  • Innovative frame-less motorcycle architecture

Facts

Despite being dyslexic he engineered world-beating motorcycles from his garage in Christchurch.

Impacts

Demonstrates that high-performance engineering can come from small-scale inventors; adds to Oceania's narrative of grassroots innovation.

W. A. S. Butement

August 18 1904 - January 25 1990, New Zealand

Inventions:

  • Proximity fuse for artillery and defence systems
  • Secure radio communications sets (Wireless Set No. 10 & No. 11) for military use
  • Over-the-horizon radar research leadership (Jindalee system)

Facts

Born in New Zealand but became the first Chief Defence Scientist of Australia.

Impacts

Improved defence and radar technologies in the region, contributing to advanced military engineering in Oceania.
